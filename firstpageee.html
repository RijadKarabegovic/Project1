Olympic sport Badminton Popularity
Badminton entered the official program of the Olympic Games in Barcelona in 1992. It is the second most popular recreational sport in the world (just behind football) and is practiced by over 200 million people. Badminton is a competitive sport, also a standard Olympic sport, played in five categories: singles women and men, women's and men's doubles, and mixed doubles. If the latest research results are to be believed, badminton has become Britain's favorite racquet-playing sport! This activity became so popular that it even eliminated tennis from the first place.

Bon Ton

You should know it, but do not have to be strictly observed:


  1. Do not walk around the pitch when a point is played, because it can be very dangerous
  2. Shake hands with opponent before and after each match



  3. Don't give advice in the middle of points
  4. Do your best, respect your opponent and never underestimate him, as this can come back to you very quickly
  5. Give the ball to your opponent when you hit the net, in case you don't do it say "sorry"
  6. If you do not know where the ball fell, it is better to repeat the point. If the ball has fallen close to the line then it is better to give the ball to the opponent than let the opponent think you are cheating
  7. No swearing and loud shouting, admittedly it can be sympathetic in moderation.
  8. In mix pairs, by unwritten rule, a woman is always serve first
  9. Clothing says a lot about the player himself, in the past badminton as well as tennis was a "white"
  10. Never step on a ball even if it is in the poorest condition. Balls should be guarded, balls are our treasure.
  11. There is no "sorry" when you hit the line or net, that is what coaches are saying; It used to be normal to say "sorry" or just raise your hand to show that you are apologizing
  12. Throwing a racquet is not appropriate
  13. No touching or hitting the net, people in the hall will look at you weired
  14. Do not serve above the first rib or high serve, as the opponent may get angry
  15. The player is obliged to provide the balls for play and each player comes to the field with his own ball. If the ball is destroyed during the match, the ball that is left unused is awarded to the player to whom the ball points after hitting the net and falls on the court
  16. If all the pitches are occupied, go to the pitch you want to check in and (between points) sign up as the next player on the pitch.
  17. If you are playing on the field and you already have a player / players registered for the next match, give your place to new players. Usually people check in and the winner remains. However, if you have played 3 matches in a row, consider a break :)
  18. Everything is allowed within tolerance limits, still it's just a game, smile on your face and enjoy !!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!

School of Badminton


The SaBad badminton playroom "MiniTon" is intended for children aged 5-12 years.

Designed as a playroom with an emphasis on the development of basic motor skills, with the addition of badminton as the most interesting game in the world




The SaBad Badminton MiniTon is a great beginner sport for kids.

Improves motor development and movement coordination, positively affects precision and agility.

In addition to mastering basic techniques, these trainings also aim to instill sporting values as well as teaching children teamwork.

What is badminton?


Badminton is an Olympic sport, extremely popular in Asia and northern Europe. The basics of badminton rules and techniques are easy to learn.

Badminton is an aerobic sport and uses the entire body musculature. Exercising badminton regularly can help preserve the heart and blood vessels, reduce cholesterol, strengthen muscles and bones, deal with stress, lose weight and have a generally positive health effect.

Official competitions are: women's and men's singles, men's, women's and mixed doubles, team.




Where is badminton played?


Badminton is purely an indoor sport

Ground dimensions are 6.1 x 13.40 m for doubles and 5.18 x 13.40 m for singles, net height is 1.55 m





Game features

Badminton is an extremely fast and dynamic sport

The game consists of a series of changes of strokes, steps, short sprints, jumps and fast changes of direction


Badminton equipment

The standard badminton equipment is a badminton racket, ball and net

Beginners and recreational players use mostly plastic balls, while competitors play and train with feathered balls

About badminton
Badminton is a racquet sport that involves either two individuals or two pairs. Players play on their side of the rectangular court divided by the net in the middle. Playershit the badminton ball with a racket so that it crosses the net and falls into the opposite half of the field, with the goal of the opponent failing to return it.
